HDB Lime Paint Residential

Terra Tone Feature Wall, 4-Room HDB

Bishan Street 24  ·  Completed Q4 2024

The client had recently obtained keys to a freshly painted BTO. The HDB standard white emulsion was flat and felt temporary — they wanted one wall in the living room that felt considered.

We specified lime paint in a warm terracotta-blush tone, applied in three coats with a dry-brush technique to create tonal depth. The wall now reads completely differently in morning versus evening light, which was exactly the brief.

"We've had visitors ask three times if we imported the paint from Italy. It's lime paint from Plain Work."

Condominium Microcement Wet Area

Seamless Wet Room, River Valley Condo

River Valley Road  ·  Completed Q3 2024

The client was renovating a 2-bedroom unit in an older development. The existing bathroom had standard ceramic tiles — grout lines, visual interruption, and a dated feel. The brief was a seamless, grout-free bathroom that could handle daily shower use.

We assessed the existing substrate and specified a full waterproofing membrane before the microcement system. Both walls and floor were treated in a single warm grey tone. The continuous surface from wall to floor — no profile, no interruption — was the result the client wanted and what they got.

"The bathroom feels twice as large now. The grout lines were cutting the room into pieces."
System Applied
Microcement — 5 coat
Area
~18 sqm (walls + floor)
Substrate
Hacked tile + waterproof membrane
Timeline
6 days incl. sealing

Lime Plaster + Patina — Commercial F&B
Commercial Lime Plaster Patina & Oxidised

Shophouse Café Interior, Keong Saik

Keong Saik Road  ·  Completed Q2 2024

A new specialty coffee concept in a conservation shophouse. The ID specified a material finish that referenced the building's age without being a pastiche of it — aged, textural, honest. The brief was no faux finishes: real materials, real texture.

We applied hand-troweled lime plaster on the main feature wall and counter face, then introduced patina and oxidised treatment to the metal shelf brackets and bar front. The result is a surface language that sits alongside the original terrazzo floor and exposed brick without competing with it.

"We spec'd mineral finishes because we knew it had to last. Three years from now it'll look better, not worse."
— Interior Designer, Atelier Luma
Systems Applied
Lime Plaster + Patina treatment
Area
~60 sqm (walls + joinery)
Substrate
Brick, plasterboard, mild steel
